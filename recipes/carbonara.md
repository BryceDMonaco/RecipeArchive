## Ingredients

- 8 oz pasta
- 55 g (1/2 cup lightly packed) fresh, fine grated Parmesan
- 30 g (1/4 cup lightly packed) fresh, fine grated Pecorino
- 4 oz guanciale, pancetta, or unsmoked bacon (guanciale is authentic, but difficult to find)
- 1 large egg
- 1 large egg yolk
- Crushed black pepper, to taste
- Salt, to taste

## Instructions

1. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook pasta according to package instructions.
2. Allow eggs to sit outside of fridge while grating cheese.
3. Slice meat into 1/2 inch wide strips. Cook on medium low with no extra oil - it will cook in its own fat. Leave meat in pan when done, do not drain oil/fat.
4. In a mixing bowl, whisk the egg and extra egg yolk. Add all cheese and whisk together to make a paste. Whisk in crushed pepper to taste.
5. When pasta is done cooking, save 1/2 cup of pasta water, then strain the rest.
6. Transfer pasta to the bacon pan, toss the pasta to coat in the fat.
7. Transfer pasta and bacon to the egg mixing bowl, add a small splash of pasta water, and stir with the handle of a spoon or spatula until sauce turns creamy. Be very conservative with adding water - do not let the sauce get runny.
8. Serve with extra cheese on top, salt and pepper to taste.

## Notes

Unsmoked/uncured bacon is very important if you decide to use bacon. Guanciale is the authentic meat if available.

The sauce comes together much better if the cheese is grated, not shredded.

Pecorino can be substituted with more Parmesan if not available, or skipped to reduce cheese.

You may need extra salt if the bacon is unsalted. Warmed bowls help to keep the pasta hot.

Leftovers lose the creamy sauce - it is best to make this fresh as needed.
