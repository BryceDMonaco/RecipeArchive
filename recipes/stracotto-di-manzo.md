---
title: Stracotto di Manzo (Beef Braised in Red Wine)
tags: italian, beef, slow-cooker
---

## Ingredients

- 1 beef roast
- Sea salt and black pepper to taste
- 3 tablespoons unrefined olive oil
- 1 large onion, diced (yellow or red)
- 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 3 to 4 garlic cloves, slivered
- 3 to 4 cups peeled, seeded, and chopped tomatoes (fresh or jarred)
- 1 3/4 cups dry red wine (375 mL / half a standard bottle)
- 2 cups beef or chicken stock
- 1 lemon zest strip (about 3 inches long)
- 1 cinnamon stick
- A few whole cloves (optional)
- Minced fresh flat leaf parsley for garnish

## Instructions

1. Tie the meat with twine if desired. Pat dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt and pepper.
2. Warm a large Dutch oven over medium heat with olive oil. Once hot, sear the meat on all sides until golden brown.
3. Remove the meat and set aside. Add extra oil if needed, then saute onions, carrots, celery, and garlic until tender. Add tomatoes and salt. Deglaze the pot, then add remaining ingredients except parsley. Bring to a boil.
4. Return meat to the Dutch oven, cover, and simmer until tender (about 2 hours stovetop, 3 hours in a 325°F oven, or 8 hours on low in a slow cooker).
5. Remove cinnamon stick, lemon zest, and cloves. Serve with sauce and vegetables, garnished with parsley.
6. For serving the next day: refrigerate meat and sauce separately overnight. Remove visible fat, slice meat, and reheat with pan juices and vegetables.
