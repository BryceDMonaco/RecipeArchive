## Ingredients

- 1 kg (35.27 oz) 00 flour (Caputo 00 flour recommended)
- 650 ml (2.7 cups) lukewarm water
- 30 g (2 tablespoons) salt
- 2 g (0.03 oz) fresh yeast OR 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
- 10 g (2.4 teaspoons) sugar
- 25 g (0.88 oz) olive oil

## Instructions

### Day 1 - Afternoon (~4pm for 6:30pm next day)

1. Weigh your mixing bowl in grams and write down the weight before adding any ingredients (makes portioning easier the next day).
2. Mix salt and water in a large bowl.
3. Add and dissolve a small amount of flour, mixing well.
4. Add another small amount of flour and mix.
5. Add yeast and dissolve completely.
6. Add another small amount of flour and mix.
7. Add sugar and mix.
8. Continue adding remaining flour in small amounts until all is incorporated. You may add up to an extra 100 ml of water as needed in the late stages of mixing.
9. Add olive oil and knead until dough comes together.
10. Cover bowl with a wet rag for 15 minutes.
11. Remove rag and cover top of bowl with plastic wrap.
12. Ferment for 24 hours sealed at room temperature, the minimum fermentation time is 16 hours.

### Day 2 - Shaping

1. Divide dough into 250g balls (should yield six plus some extra). If you know the weight of your bowl, subtract it from the current weight and divide by six for exact dough ball weights.
2. Place dough balls in floured containers with lids (Tupperware or cling wrap). Regular flour is fine for dusting.
3. Let dough balls rest at room temperature, sealed for at least 2 hours. 

Dough balls can be frozen after resting, let thaw overnight and add extra flour to the top as they thaw to make the dough balls less sticky.

### Day 2 - Cooking

1. If cooking on BBQ: After 2 hours of dough rising, set all burners to max and place pizza stone on the left side. Preheat for 1 hour.
2. On a floured surface, stretch dough using the hand-stretching technique (see video in notes).
3. Add toppings.
4. If using BBQ, set burners under the stone to minimum, leave others at max.
5. Place pizza on stone.
6. Rotate pizza every 30-60 seconds until cooked without burning.

## Notes

Add some flour and corn meal to your pizza peel to prevent the dough from sticking.

All purpose flour works but requires 48 hours to ferment and tends to have a bread taste instead of a pizza crust taste. You may also need slightly more water.
