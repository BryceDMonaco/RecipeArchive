## Ingredients

### Chicken Coating
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/8 teaspoon salt
- 1-1.25 lbs boneless skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized chunks
- 3-4 tablespoons olive or avocado oil

### Sauce
- 1/3 cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 1/2 cup water
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 3 tablespoons hoisin sauce
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1/4-1/2 teaspoon dried red pepper chili flakes

### Vegetables & Garnish
- 4-6 dried red chili peppers
- 2/3 cup roasted cashews
- 1 red bell pepper, chopped
- 1 zucchini, chopped
- Sesame seeds and green onions (optional)

### Thickening Slurry
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch
- 3 tablespoons water

See notes section for lunch meal preparation.

## Instructions

1. Coat chicken with cornstarch, salt, and pepper in a bowl or bag.
2. Whisk sauce ingredients together separately.
3. Brown chicken in oil for 2-3 minutes per side; transfer to slow cooker.
4. Pour sauce over chicken.
5. Cook on LOW 2.5-4 hours or HIGH 1.5-3 hours.
6. Mix cornstarch slurry; add to cooker with peppers, bell pepper, zucchini, and cashews.
7. Cook on HIGH another 20-30 minutes until vegetables are tender.
8. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions; serve over rice or noodles.

## Notes

For lunches, I doubled the recipe (12 servings on the original site, made into 8 lunches) and used 3 lbs chicken, doubled the water used in the corn starch slurry, used red pepper flakes in place of dried peppers, slow cooked on high for 2.5 hr then 0.5 hr.
